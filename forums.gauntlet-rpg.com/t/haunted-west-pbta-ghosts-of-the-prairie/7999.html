Haunted West (PbtA): Ghosts Of The Prairie

Gauntlet Calendar

DURATION: 3 hours

NUMBER OF PLAYERS: 4 (A seat is reserved for Lowell)

CONTENT WARNING: This is a game set after the Civil War in the American Midwest, which means it will occur in an era and environment where colonialism, imperialism, racism, and genocide were policies explicitly endorsed and carried out by the government, military, and private citizens. Right was almost exclusively determined by might/capitalism. Saloons often had brothels, so alcohol and sex workers may be part of the game. Characters might use or abuse drugs. Violence was common.

However, Haunted West is a game which explicitly works against the prevailing attitudes of the time, and none of those attitudes will be used to try to shock players. This is just the historical reality of the time, and the players can choose how or whether to engage with it. Haunted West is also a Weird West game, so the supernatural will be involved in some way.

Angel Falls was once the far edge of the frontier, protected by elements of the 9th Cavalry, stationed at Fort Wilcox. It was a buffalo boomtown, and smelled like it. When the western wind came across the plains, it brought the stench of uncountable distant corpses rotting in the prairie heat.

But that was then. Now, Angel Falls is all but a ghost town. The general store still supplies the few subsistence farmers who try to stick it out, but the barber’s chair is empty most days. The Bugle & Bison saloon is still open, but the piano bench is empty whenever someone comes calling on Red Molly. Preacher Jack decided the town’s name was an omen and lit out for a place where he might actually find something like a congregation, thus depriving the Bugle & Bison of its best customer.

The few people here are tired and weary, too broke to leave for better prospects. But at least Angel Falls is quiet. Usually. Lately, there have been rumbles on the horizon, storms that look to be forming and dissipate just as quickly. For those able - or willing - to pay attention, it’s obvious that something is coming.
