jesseross November 4, 2019, 11:02am #66

Congrats on running Gold for the first time, and extra congrats for doing on a livestream! You’re braver than me!

I’ll check out the recording and give you more specific feedback later on, but based on how you described what you did, you made the right call.

If they were looking for clues, that’s definitely a Hunt Roll. And following up a Hunt Roll with a Risk Roll or Combat Roll is also the right call in a lot of cases. Hunt Rolls shouldn’t directly affect Ruin, so if they’re about to do something dangerous, then call for one of those other two rolls.

Baso (Ruin 2) is exploring the room for secret passages. The GM calls for a Hunt Roll. Baso gets a 2 as their highest. Something bad happens: a trap is triggered and a volley of arrows are flying toward Baso! The GM calls for a Risk Roll to avoid them, but their body is at risk, so they need to include a dark die. Baso rolls a 5… on their dark die. Baso avoids the arrows, but their Ruin still goes up to 3 and there’s a complication. As the complication, the GM decides that in the dive to avoid getting hit, Baso twists their ankle and takes the Condition “Limping”.

In this case, a Hunt Roll failure leads directly to the Risk Roll. Had the trap been a wondering monster, then it could have lead to a Combat Roll instead if the players decided to engage with the monster, or a Risk Roll if they just wanted to get away.

Original Trophy is all about the Risk Roll, but in Trophy Gold, almost everything that happens flows out of a Hunt Roll.

jesseross December 6, 2019, 4:41pm #68

You always start with 1 Ruin. If you pick a single Ritual, your Ruin begins at 2.

You study the spellbooks to learn the Rituals – narratively, you don’t need the spellbook and once a Ritual is learned it can’t be lost, even if it’s one you learned after character creation. The reason your Ruin doesn’t go up is purely mechanical, so that you can learn more Rituals without running out of Ruin, since there are only 6 Ruin slots to play with.

You can’t participate in a Combat Roll without a weapon – you could make a Risk Roll instead, however, or help set up your fellow treasure-hunters to help them reduce a monster’s Endurance.
