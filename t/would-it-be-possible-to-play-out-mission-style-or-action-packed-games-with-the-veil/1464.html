shane March 29, 2019, 4:07pm #3

Caveat: I have not read or played The Sprawl.

From what I know and have heard from people who have played The Sprawl it is really great at giving the mission/betrayal feel of first wave Cyberpunk. That is what it is tightly designed to do. And AFAIK it really enforces that structure of missions mechanically.

The Veil (and Cascade and the forthcoming Inheritance) are designed to cover a broader spectrum of the questions raised by Cyberpunk (over its various waves). You could do mission stuff in it - the system won’t fight you but it won’t give you a ton of mechanical support though. (Outside of some dealing harm type moves.) But if you can support the style yourself in the fiction you’d be fine.

In the Veil campaign I was in we had a little bit of mission/action stuff but it certainly wasn’t what we the players were really focused on. But those parts mostly worked and I think they would rock with players who wanted to do that stuff.

I certainly think @frasersimons would have some thoughts on the subject though…
