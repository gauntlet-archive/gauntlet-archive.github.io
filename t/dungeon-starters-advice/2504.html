Here‚Äôs what I have written in Homebrew World (for those unfamiliar), and I stand by it!

Establish Premise
Before character creation, work with the players to establish two things:

  • Where they are (and it should be someplace interesting and fantastic)
  • What they are doing there, in broad strokes (exploring, rescuing, searching, hunting, fleeing; something exciting, adventurous, desperate)

Here‚Äôs a good level of detail: ‚Äúyou‚Äôre on the run from something scary, and it‚Äôs chased you into the dreaded Obsidian Forests of Yend.‚Äù

Hook Questions
While they make characters, formulate some hook questions. These should both assert something and prompt the players to make up details. E.g. ‚ÄúWhat are you fleeing, that you were desperate enough to enter the Obsidian Forests of Yend?‚Äù

Your hook questions should establish all of these:

How do you calibrate stakes in your games, fantastic or otherwise?
