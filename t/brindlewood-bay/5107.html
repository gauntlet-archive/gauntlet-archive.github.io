This is a nine-session run of Brindlewood Bay in May/June on Sunday evenings (BST). Brindlewood Bay is a game of cozy mystery investigation and lurking horror by Jason Cordova. It was published as a standalone project for the Codex zine for Gauntlet patreon supporters, and is now available for purchase. Sessions will be three hours in length, and we will play using Zoom. This game will be recorded, and may be released on Youtube with the consent of all participants.

This game will be played using the open table policy, so you are free to sign up for as many or few sessions as able. Signing up for all sessions is strongly encouraged, but is not required. The premise for the game should allow for characters to drop in and out, though it is designed more for slow-burn investigation rather than episodic play. For this reason I imagine the play experience will be better if you can commit to the full run of the game, or at least a few consecutive sessions. If you do sign up for a session but are unable to make it, please let me know, and remove yourself from the sign-up so that whoever is on the waitlist will be notified.

Brindlewood Bay is inspired by ‚Äúcozy mystery‚Äù media, such as Murder She Wrote, though with an added element of lurking eldritch horror. So, imagine Miss Marple but flavoured with Twin Peaks and The Wicker Man. In Brindlewood Bay, you will all play retired women in the eponymous town - a small whaling town turned tourist trap in New England - who are members of a mystery book club and get embroiled in murder investigations around their home town. As they investigate these seemingly unrelated murders, they may learn deeper secrets pointing to occult forces working behind the scenes of the seaside town.

Content Warning: While the game - especially at the outset - will have a cozy tone, it is ultimately a game about murder, so some themes of violence will be inevitable. While not a high-action game, there is risk to the characters, which can extend to risk to their lives. Likewise, while it is unlikely to form a large part of the game, romance and sexuality are explicitly referenced as part of the characters. There is also a strong theme of supernatural horror, which will become more prevalent as the game progresses, with the possibility for personal and body horror. We will have a discussion on tone and content before the game begins, as well as establishing Lines & Veils to provide a comfortable play experience. This game will take place under the Gauntlet Community Code of Conduct and the safety tools Script Change and X-Card will be in use.
