I agree with both those factors but think that are many more:

  1. Skill specificity - In a game with very broad skills, your character might be effective at things you don‚Äôt expect them to. If you made a character with a high Strength because they are a laborer, are they also good at jousting?   2. Skill clarity - In a game with lots of specific skills, your character might miss a skill during character creation that you really want them to have.

  3. Expectations clash with character creation rules - A new player wants to create a character who is a master wizard who runs a potion shop, but has a fierce rivalry with a competitor.
