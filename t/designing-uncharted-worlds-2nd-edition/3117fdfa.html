I‚Äôd greatly appreciate feedback on this new version of the Academic career Move. This is a tough one to get right, because the ‚ÄúAcademic‚Äù sci-fi trope is (ironically) both very wide in subject matter, and very focused in tone and methodology.

PATCH UP ‚Äì Academic Move

When you treat a living subject or reprogram an open system, Roll+Intellect.
When you rewire, calibrate, or overclock a piece of technology, Roll+Expertise.

On a 10+ , choose one:

  • Diagnostic: Discover what‚Äôs hurting, draining, or otherwise afflicting the subject.
  • Boost: Choose a limit to the subject‚Äôs actions. Temporarily ignore that limitation.
  • Lock: Choose a behavior. The subject is temporarily unable to behave that way.

On a 7-9 , choose one (as a 10+ result), but you‚Äôll have to cause harm to the subject, suffer harm yourself, and/or sacrifice resources to get results.

On a 6-, the situation, procedure, or subject becomes incredibly volatile.

Diagnostic is pretty straight forward, and is there as a better version of Seek the Truth, but only for health assessment. See: every doctor, biologist, and engineer on every sci-fi show. Spouting exposition of ‚Äúwhat‚Äôs wrong with X‚Äù is like 90% of their job.

I wanted to give a certain amount of creative leeway with the the Boost and Lock.

Boost is the ‚Äúmake things work again‚Äù. It can be used to tinker with a gun to punch through armor with its next shot (ignore limitation: can‚Äôt pierce heavy armor), coax a shorted out vehicle engine to start up (ignore limitation: overloaded), or get a soldier on their feet (ignore limitation: debilitating injuries).

Lock can sedate people (unable to stay awake), prevent overloads (unable to gain more charge), or just lock out people from systems (unable to accept new users). I worry it might be a bit too open to abuse, especially when dealing with living beings (‚Äúunable to continue breathing‚Äù), but the necessary fictional positioning (treating, programming, calibrating) will hopefully act as a natural deterrent. Still, the expanded rules will probably highlight this issue, and advise the GM to set certain expectations of ‚Äúfair play‚Äù and making a fun story. Which applies to the whole game, really, since player authorship means everything is at least somewhat open to abuse.

Also I am totally not married to the name ‚ÄúPatch Up‚Äù. If anything, it breaks the ‚Äúone word verb‚Äù naming convention I‚Äôve got going on. Ah, this is a very interesting topic that comes up a lot in PbtA. Allowing characters the freedom to act vs niche protection.

The Core Moves are written as situational Moves. It‚Äôs less about ‚Äúwhat‚Äù you want to do (boost a computer‚Äôs power) and more ‚Äúwhy‚Äù you want to do it (do it before time runs out, do it to discover a way forward, do it to brain-burn the enemy hacker). ‚ÄúBoosting the power‚Äù isn‚Äôt the end goal in that case, but rather the means you use to achieve a goal (i.e.: Roll + Intellect).

Basically, non-Academics boost computers, administer drugs, re-calibrate machinery in order to solve immediate problems.
Academics do those things to create and control scenarios, and set up long-term advantageous situations.

The niche protection of Careers over Core Moves is two-fold:

  • Predictable results: While both an Academic and non-Academic can boost a power relay or drug a guard, the Academic has more leeway and creative interpretation with their Move. In the same way that anyone can pilot a ship, but an Explorer can pull off amazing stunts.
  • Better results: The Core Moves have harsher and more frequent costs. Even at the highest level of success, it usually costs something to get the job done. Career Moves are professional versions of the amateur fumbling. A 10+ on a Career Move means full success at least, if not a little bonus on top. Anyone can try to sneak into somewhere, but the Clandestine can slip in and scan the area or pick up a bit of bonus loot.

Took a bit of time off before re-evaluating the new Academic Move, taking the feedback into account of course.

As I mentioned, making an ‚ÄòAcademic‚Äô move is hard because it covers such a wide spectrum of non-overlapping disciplines. And while I still kinda like Patch-Up/Operate, I feel that it doesn‚Äôt quite express what Academic career should be.

So I went back to the drawing board and considered three big things:

  • What does ‚ÄúAcademic‚Äù mean in a sci-fi context when it isn‚Äôt a doctor? That was a big issue, because the ‚Äòobvious‚Äô solution is to pattern it off Julian Bashir and Simon Tam.
  • What do the various scientific fields have in common? Where do their methods, drives, or ways of thinking overlap?
  • What does this Move provide to the other careers? How does it help other Moves?

I ended up finding my inspiration in Dan Abnett‚Äôs Eisenhorn (omnibus edition), a fantastic read that I strongly recommend. In it, one of the titular character‚Äôs associates is Uber Aemos, a ‚ÄúSavant‚Äù. Basically a font of knowledge, history, logic, translation, and observation. Aemos really feels like a ‚Äòpure‚Äô Academic, both literally and in UW terms.

From there I looked at the ‚ÄúExpertise‚Äù mirror of that Intellect. Expertise is hands-on, dirt-under-the-fingernails science, all about objects, tech, terrain. It got me thinking of shell casings, ballistics, autopsies, ruins, etc. Which brought me to archaeologists (Indiana Jones) and crime scene investigations (Sherlock Holmes), and cemented the idea.

The overlap between various scientific fields is the ability to study and analyze. Sounds obvious, but making that into a Move was tricky.

So here it is:

STUDY ‚Äì Academic Move

When you to make an assessment from research, samples, or scans, Roll+Intellect.
When you piece together clues from debris, remains, or markings, Roll+Expertise.

On a 10+ , choose an inquiry. The GM will prompt you to share your findings:

  • Diagnosis: What‚Äôs currently wrong with this, and how to deal with it.
  • Sequencing: The history or chain of events that happened here.
  • Provenance: Where did this originate? What was the source or cause?
  • Extrapolation: Which way is this going? What can be expected next?

On a 7-9 , choose an inquiry (as a 10+ result), but the GM gets to add an unfortunate detail, complication, hook, or twist to your findings.

On a 6-, the situation, subject, or results prove to be quite volatile.

And that‚Äôs what the Academic offers to other careers: the ability to learn about something in order to make better decisions. Military Academics will use it to predict troop movements. Academic Explorers can delve into the history of alien ruins, or find out what kind of creature left these tracks. Clandestine Academics can track down murderers, etc.

I think this may be the way to go. This way healing/medicine can be a Skill of the Academic (as characters level up and specialize) while repairs and calibrations remain the purview of the Industrial (though Industrial Academics still make the best repairs because they can assess/diagnose a problem, and solve it efficiently.)

I ended up taking a bit of time to revisit the Careers, and tightened up the three careers that weren‚Äôt quite distinct enough for my tastes. Hopefully this will lead to less overlap, and less uncertainty about which stat applies to which action.

COUNCIL (Advocate)
