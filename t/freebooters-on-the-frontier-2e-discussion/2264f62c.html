Here‚Äôs my proposed solution to one little problem with West Marches in FotF.

Problem

  1. World Creation is a great bit of Session zero, and is a great way to get everyone invested in the setting.
  2. West Marches play expects 10+ players.
  3. World Creation in FotF with 10 players feels like it might get‚Ä¶ long; and boring.
  4. Also, not everyone will be at Session Zero, and so therefore may not be as invested in the setting.

Solution: in the first session, we will create the 2-3 Lost Civilizations. We will only create a new civilization if the narrative calls for it; or if we get an 8th+ player. (8th is just a number I came up with. Feels right).

Every session thereafter, if we get new players, the new players get to add one new Region and one new Landmark to the map. GM(s) will track the regions and landmarks associated with each player, and be sure to include something related to one or more of those Frontier features in that session‚Äôs play.

With that, I think I‚Äôm ready to launch into a West Marches FotF game.

Comments on Pass the Night. Note, I haven‚Äôt been tracking this move in the past, so not sure what iterations have come before. But I had some thoughts.

First off, not clear who should roll this move. Does each player roll? Does each player roll during their watch? I think maybe it should be stated that if you roll +Safety, that is a roll that applies to the entire party, and the party should select someone to roll. In fact, you could adjust the move a bit to say ‚ÄúPass the Night When the party settles into sleep, take note of the watch order, and someone in the party rolls +safety. Second - seems like it‚Äôs pretty tough to get a good night‚Äôs sleep, even when you are in a safe area. So that means out of 10 nights of travel, they will only regain HP on average 1-2 nights (assuming they have food the night before). Third - I can see Pass the Night being a pick-list
