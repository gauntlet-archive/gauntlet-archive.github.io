My first incursion, Oath and Folly, set in a desert. Inspired by Napoleon’s failed 1798 incursion to Egypt, but set a bit more in a swords and sorcery era.

Both a lot of fun and a lot of work to write, but that hearty mélange of work and fun is where the good stuff arises.

I adapted Trophy’s six characteristics in the Bestiary a bit. The six characteristics of monsters are based on Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs. They are still behaviors, but are couched in a stratification of needs. Generally speaking they conform to this:

  1. Biological (food, drink)
  2. Biological (shelter)
  3. Safety (tasks, security)
  4. Belonging (packs, orders, or family)
  5. Esteem (status, recognition, freedom)
  6. Self-actualization (individual needs or drives)

Some pack or hive monsters and those lacking sentience may not have individual needs.

Realizing that I never added the link to my incursion once the folder was closed.

The incursion is “The Mithril Mines of Airgead Canyon”.

In some ways the incursion is “Trophy Lite” with the horror turned down, but a GM could easily turn up the horror and suspense if they wanted to.

Airgead Canyon is a very deep canyon about one day’s travel from the nearest town. At the bottom of the canion lies an old and abandoned Mithril Mine reported to be filled with mithril ripe for the taking. The theme is Hubris and it’s designed as a one-shot intro incursion for new Trophy Gold players that plays on some classic RPG tropes.
