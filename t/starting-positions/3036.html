Flashbacks are a very useful tool. As well as allowing you to start with the action and fill in the back story later, they‚Äôre also fun to allow players to fill out their characters‚Äô back story once play is already underway (which is often better than doing it up-front before players have even met their characters).

In our most recent Dungeon World campaign, we really went to town on the flashbacks, starting every session with one. Sometimes they were flashbacks to earlier moments in the characters‚Äô lives. Sometimes they were to completely different places and times, filing in gaps in the overall story ‚Äî and on a few memorable occasions sending it spinning off in completely new directions.

Some games (blades in the dark?) mechanise this by starting in media res and then allowing players to call for flashback scenes when they realise they should have prepared something.
