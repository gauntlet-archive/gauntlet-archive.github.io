This is a series of eight sessions, running for 3 hours each, of Godsend by Khelren, a World of Legacy based on Legacy: Life Among the Ruins by Jay Iles. Godsend is a game of world-shaping divine power. You will play a pantheon of gods, conspiring to save the world from armageddon, or bring it about; and you will also play the avatar of another god, a once-mortal agent empowered with great power. Like Legacy, Godsend is a PbtA game, though it is diceless. We will play using the Jitsi browser-based chat tool. This game will be recorded, and may be released on Youtube with the consent of all participants.

Due to the format of the game - with each player representing one god, and the avatar of another player’s god - it is preferable for players to be able to commit to all eight sessions if at all possible. The game will still be run as an open table game, and where possible I will draw players from the wait list to fill any gaps as they arise. But I think the game will play best with as much continuity as possible. Please note, there will be no session on 10th March.

Content Warning: This game is about gods and their followers, so religious themes are inevitable. Powers operate at large scale, and the game ultimately deals with existential threats, so the potential for wide-scale death and destruction is possible. Finally, intra-player conflict can be a significant part of this game - both in the power relationships between gods and their avatars, and in rivalries between gods and avatars more generally. We will have a discussion on tone and content before the game begins, as well as establishing Lines & Veils to provide a comfortable play experience. This game will take place under the Gauntlet Inclusivity Policy and the safety tools Script Change and X-Card will be in use.
