Safety tools have a function that supersedes the way the rules are worded, which is, as @William_Nichols put it in another thread, making sure the players feel more important than the game. If a player is clearly using one of those tools in bad faith, to do something other than protect the safety of themselves or others at the table, having a conversation about their behavior is not only permissible, but necessary.

There are rules about climbing ropes to keep people safe off the ground, but if somebody is using your climbing rope to tie somebody up, the rules might change a bit.
