Wilhelm August 15, 2019, 10:52am #34

As a player
During the summer we played my Gnostic horror OSR game Kuf. A friend is writing a campaign setting called The Landlocked North, set in rural Sweden. He’s running/play testing that. And I quite enjoy sitting on the player side of the screen in his slow burning Twin Peakesque horror mystery. Continuing exploring those mysteries would be my first choice for Autumn gaming, and it seems I’ll be able to do just that. Good times.

As a GM
My GM heart is fickle. A week ago it was set on running a cyberpunk game using rules and setting elements from Kuf. But as of two or three days I’m planning a … strange … mega dungeon experience set around a flying castle. I’ve even made covers for both ideas, in case they ever see so much play that I decide to write them down as modules for whatever games they might go well with. I’m sure I’ll be changing my mind on a weekly to bi-weekly basis until I actually have some players gathered around my table and we’re rolling up characters.
