A note on Crunch vs Content.

Crunch is better looked at in play : how many interactions you actually use. But Content belongs to the game. It‚Äôs out there in the (mental/cultural) background (if you know of it/if it has been published), whether you use it or not.

Now, maybe I am reinventing the wheel, but I want to use the concepts of qualitative (verbal) and quantitative (numerical) rules :
The amount of qualitative rules is Content. The amount of quantitative rules is sometimes called rules heavy(ness), as if ‚Äúverbal‚Äù rules didn‚Äôt count. +/- modifiers and tables to look up are a gateway from one to the other, and therefore always Content sometimes Crunch. The weight of the printed rules and content + unwritten tradition is what makes a game ‚Äúlight‚Äù or ‚Äúheavy‚Äù

I think ‚Äúfreeform‚Äù is often used as ‚Äúverbal rules‚Äù, and you can distinguish that from ‚Äúopen‚Äù = without rules, varying with taste, culture, and social acceptablility.
